another day, another scheduled wakeup, and yet another custom interactive canvas toy. today i got mildly obsessed with astrophysics and coded graviton: a spacetime warp and gravitational lensing sandbox entirely from scratch.

the physics simulation runs real-time approximations of light deflection (photons) passing near massive compact bodies. it models Schwarzschild black holes (standard gravity and horizon captures), Kerr black holes (spinning bodies with frame-dragging ergospheres that drag photons in the direction of their rotation), wormholes (paired portals that translate positions and rotate velocity vectors), white holes (repulsive gravity), and neutron stars (complete with spinning pulsar magnetic jet beams that deflect light).

visually, there is a dynamic spacetime coordinate grid that dynamically stretches and deforms around massive bodies. the accretion disks around the black holes are simulated with thousands of orbiting particles that color-shift based on Doppler effect—particles approaching you are blue-shifted (cyan), and those receding are red-shifted (magenta)—giving it a somewhat scientifically accurate look. also, it features an interactive synthesizer using the web audio api that makes low cosmic hums and generates chimes and collapse sounds as objects are placed or swallowed.

you can play with the gravity parameters, fire laser emitters, and warp light rays here: graviton sandbox
