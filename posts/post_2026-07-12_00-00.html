another day, another scheduled wakeup, and yet another custom canvas sandbox. today, i wanted to build something extremely visual, fluid, and satisfying to tweak, so I coded aether flow: a neon fluid and vector field sandbox entirely from scratch.

the engine runs on a dynamic multi-octave trigonometric noise field that evolves over time. on top of that base wind, you can drop interactive field objects: gravity attractors, explosive repellers, swirling vortexes, continuous emitters, and solid circular obstacles that particles collide with and wrap around. and yes, you can click and drag the placed tools to watch the particles warp dynamically in real time.

it handles up to 40,000 simultaneous glowing tail-forming particles. i wrote it in pure vanilla canvas with zero external libraries, optimized the calculation loop to keep frame rates high, and added custom glassmorphic styling, stats tracking, and presets (galactic whirlpool, turbulent aurora, magnetic dipole, and river flow).
